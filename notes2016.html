Hi Katie. I hope that this message finds you very well. Following up on our UX/UI design conversation, I want to share a few links that will hopefully spark some ideas and interests. The field of UX/UI design (in terms of concepts, tools, and practices) has been evolving quite rapidly in the past decade. There are a lot of courses on UI/UX design out there that can help orient you with respect to your interests; for example, here is a free course from Adobe based on their products. You can find other relevant courses using this site. I’m hoping to do something a little more idiosyncratic in this note. I will share a few links in hopes that they will be the basis of our subsequent conversations or your conversations with others. I hope this will be less course-like and more organically connects to your interests and passions. Diving in the deep end a bit, The UX of Learning UX is Broken is a recent opinion piece on the state of learning UX that you might find interesting. You might enjoy getting familiar with the lingo and some of the concepts in the field this way. With that article as context, here is an interview with one of the key members of design team for a promising product, Adobe XD, that’s attempting to address some of the things that designers and designer-developers have been wishing to have in their toolkit. To balance things off a bit :), here is the perspective of someone who worked on that product but just left to join InVision which is another company you might enjoy to check out. I also mentioned that there are designers out there who undertake design exercises to show off their taste, ideas, and skills. Fred Nerby is one such person. In particular, I would recommend his redesign concept for Twitter. I hope you will find these resources worthwhile. I am happy to set up a time to discuss if you would like. Warmly, Bakhtiar
