Final Project

On April 14th, 2015, I met with two of my instructors to review some HTML basics and I built my first "Hello World" page seen in the screenshot below.

I became frustrated with my inability to align text or divs where I wanted them to be. We happened to be using px as the measure and I asked, "Isn't there a pixel ruler I could use?" If I were laying out a page in the physical world, I would grab a ruler to find the distance I needed to move the element for proper alignment on the page. This is where screen-based design and I struggled. It was not tangible and I had no control.

Two days after this meeting, I emailed my instructors asking them if I could switch my final project and create an on-screen pixel ruler. I was more excited about this idea than anything else and my instructors approved my request. Then commenced a very intense month of design and build. The project was heavy on the JS, you can find snippits and all the tests I ran at my pixel ruler repository. For my final presentation, I walked my peers through the why and how. I also shared the final product, a bookmarklet and the JS.

Next Steps

I was extremely proud to create a digital version of a tool from the physical world. I may not have that much control over the language and the syntax of code, and if I stay in this field, I won't be able to control that, but I know now that I can digitally build tools to control the affect of those things.

There were still some tweaks I needed to do on the ruler. The color palette is less than desirable, but overall, I work out the kinks and functionality of the design so someone could begin using it immediately. I had high hopes for Gittish. It is a project that resonates with me because of my personal relationship and struggle with language. I also know I am not the only person who may feel alienated from the tech world. I believe it is time for new blood to enter the field and bring with it new perspective. I would love to help facilitate that in some way.

At this time, I believed that I needed to get more experience using the languages that I would one day like to manipulate, so my immediate plans focused on making more.
